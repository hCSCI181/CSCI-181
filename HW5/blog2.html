5 Lessons You'll Learn on the Road

Travel can be a life-altering experience, even more so when it is done solo or with ample time to reflect. Here are 5 lessons that we learned from living a nomadic lifestyle.

  1. Solo time is essential:
    Taking time for oneself is essential to living a productive lifestyle. After traveling solo, I had a better idea of my needs and desires thanks to the time I took to reflect while traveling.
  2. Mistakes and breakdowns are part of the journey:
    When beginning to travel, a lot of us try to do everything above and beyond our power to make sure everything goes just right. What we learned on the road is that hiccups in travel are an inevitable part of the journey and are such a valuable learning experiecence.
  3. You don't need all the perfect gear:
    A lot of time travel of all kinds (van travel, globtrotting, thru-hiking, backpacking, and more) are few as elitist activities that can only be done by those who have all the best gear. But, in reality, we learned that a simple $20 cooler from Walmart worked just as well as a $1000 fridge installed in a van home and that a simple camera captures priceless moments just as well as a professional grade Nikon. If you don't have all the "right" gear, just go!
  4. Friendships are invaluable:
    The friendships you'll make on the road will make the journey 100x better. Be open to new friendships and stay curious. It's likely you'll meet friends for life while traveling
  5. You are your best friend:
    Living on the road often means living alone. This means that you'll be relying solely on yourself for all your needs and entertainment at times. Enjoy yourself. On the road, you'll learn that you are you own best friend.

2018 Reading List for Adventurists

Reading can be a wonderful form of adventure inspiration and a way to visit far-off places when you can't be traveling yourself. We here at Women on the Road community know that it can be difficult to find books great adventure themed books written by women, so we've compiled a list of books that we love and think you will too.

Wild by Cheryl Strayed

This book is amazing and if you have read it yet, you definitely should. Join Cheryl Strayed as she walks the PCT from Mexico to Cananda in the wake of her mother's death

Girl in the Woods by Aspen Matis

In this memoir, Aspen Matis tells her story of thru-hiking, both of how she did it, what she carried, and what it gave her. We think you'll love this one. **Trigger Warning: ** Subject matter explicitly discusses sexual assault.

Beyond the Earth and Sky by Jamie Zeppa

Jamie Zeppa writes about her experience living in Tibet for 3 years in a way that truly captures the spirit of solo travel.
